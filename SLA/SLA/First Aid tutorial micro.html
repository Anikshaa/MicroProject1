What is First Aid?

A first aid tutorial teaches immediate care for injuries or illnesses before professional medical help arrives, focusing on core principles like ensuring safety, calling for help, and managing the most critical issues first, often summarized by the ABCs of first aid: Airway, Breathing, and Circulation.
Learning to assess the scene, protect yourself, and then provide care for common problems such as bleeding, burns, and choking can save lives and promote recovery.

History of First Aid

1. Ancient and Early Origins

First aid has roots going back thousands of years:
Ancient Egypt, Greece, and Rome documented methods for treating wounds, fractures, and snakebites.
Greek physicians like Hippocrates described techniques for bandaging and cleaning wounds.
Roman military medics used splints, tourniquets, and field treatments for injured soldiers.
These early approaches focused mostly on battlefield injuries, since war was the most common place where urgent care was needed.
