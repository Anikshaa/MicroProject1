WHAT IS CPR?

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) is an emergency procedure that combines chest compressions and rescue breaths to keep blood flowing to the brain and other vital organs until professional medical help arrives.
Time is critical; immediate action significantly increases the chance of survival.

Key Steps for CPR (Adult/Adolescent)

1.Check the Scene and the Person:

a.Ensure the area is safe for you and the victim.
b.Tap the person's shoulder and shout, "Are you okay?" to check for responsiveness.

2.Call for Help:

i.If the person is unresponsive and not breathing (or only gasping), immediately call your local emergency number (e.g., 911) or assign someone nearby to do it.
ii.If an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) is available, retrieve it or have someone else get it.

3.Position and Open the Airway:

i.Gently roll the person onto their back on a firm, flat surface.
ii.Use the head-tilt, chin-lift maneuver to open the airway: tilt the head back slightly by pushing on the forehead while lifting the chin with two fingers.

4.Check for Breathing:

i.Look, listen, and feel for normal breathing for no more than 10 seconds. Occasional gasps are not considered normal breathing.
ii.If the person is not breathing normally, start compressions.

5.Start Chest Compressions (Circulation):

i.Kneel beside the person.
ii.Place the heel of one hand in the center of the chest (lower half of the sternum). Place your other hand on top of the first, interlocking your fingers.
iii.Keep your arms straight and position your shoulders directly over your hands.
iv.Push hard and fast at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute (think of the beat to "Stayin' Alive").
v.Compress the chest at least 2 inches (5 cm) deep.
vi.Allow the chest to recoil completely between compressions.
vii.Deliver a cycle of 30 compressions.

6.Give Rescue Breaths (Airway/Breathing):

i.After 30 compressions, perform the head-tilt, chin-lift again.
ii.Pinch the nose shut, take a normal breath, and cover the person's mouth tightly with yours.
iii.Blow in for about 1 second, watching for the chest to rise.
iv.Give a second rescue breath.
Note: If you are untrained or unwilling to give rescue breaths, perform hands-only CPR (continuous chest compressions).

7.Continue:

Continue cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths until the person shows signs of movement, an AED becomes available, or EMS takes over.
